Bangladesh (officially the People's Republic of Bangladesh) is a South Asian country known for its rich history, dense population, and unique geography.
Key Facts Capital and Largest City: Dhaka Official Language: Bengali (Bangla) Population: Over 170 million (eighth-most populous country in the world) Area: Approximately 148,460 square kilometers (57,320 sq mi) Government: Unitary parliamentary republic Currency: Bangladeshi Taka (BDT) National Anthem: "Amar Shonar Bangla" (My Golden Bengal), written by Rabindranath Tagore National Day: March 26 (Independence Day) Borders: Shares land borders with India on three sides (north, west, and east) and Myanmar to the southeast. It has a coastline on the Bay of Bengal to the south.
Geography and Climate Riverine Delta: Bangladesh is located in the fertile Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta, a low-lying, riverine plain formed by the confluence of the Padma (Ganges), Jamuna (Brahmaputra), and Meghna rivers Rivers: The country is crisscrossed by a vast network of rivers, which are vital for agriculture and transportation but also make the country prone to seasonal flooding. Highest Point: Mowdok Taung in the Chittagong Hill Tracts. Largest Mangrove Forest: Home to the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage site, which is also the home of the Royal Bengal Tiger. Climate: The country has a tropical monsoon climate with a hot, humid summer and a cooler, dry winter. Heavy rainfall occurs during the monsoon season.
History Ancient Period: The region has a history stretching back over four millennia, with a succession of Buddhist and Hindu kingdoms. Sultanate and Mughal Rule: Following the Muslim conquest in the 13th century, the region saw various periods of Muslim rule, becoming a prominent and wealthy province under the Bengal Sultanate and later the Mughal Empire. British Rule: The British East India Company took control after the Battle of Plassey in 1757, leading to a long period of British colonial rule that ended in 1947 with the partition of British India. Partition of India (1947): The region became East Bengal, a geographically non-contiguous part of the newly formed Pakistan, later renamed East Pakistan. Liberation War (1971): Due to political and economic disparities and linguistic differences, a strong sense of Bengali nationalism grew, leading to the Bangladesh Liberation War in 1971. After a nine-month war, Bangladesh achieved independence on December 16, 1971, with the surrender of the Pakistani forces.
Economy and Culture Economy: Bangladesh has a rapidly growing economy. The ready-made garment (RMG) industry is the country's largest export sector. Other key industries include textiles, pharmaceuticals, jute products, and ceramics. Culture: The culture is a mix of Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic traditions, with a strong emphasis on music, dance, literature, and festivals. The Bengali language is a central part of the national identity, and the Bengali Language Movement of 1952 is a significant event in the nation's history. Cuisine: Known for its flavorful cuisine, particularly fish (especially hilsa, the national fish), rice, and various spices. Bangladesh is a developing country that has made remarkable progress in recent decades in areas like poverty reduction, education, and healthcare, but it continues to face challenges such as political instability and the significant impacts of climate change.
